About Sabah

Sabah, known as "The Land Below the Wind," is a Malaysian state located on the northern portion of Borneo, the third largest island in the world.

Sabah is renowned for its spectacular biodiversity, with lush rainforests that are among the oldest in the world, majestic mountains including Mount Kinabalu (the highest peak in Southeast Asia), and pristine islands with some of the best diving spots globally like Sipadan Island.

The state is home to diverse ethnic groups, each with their own unique cultures, traditions, and languages. Sabah's capital, Kota Kinabalu, is a vibrant city that blends modernity with traditional elements.

Learn more about Sabah on Wikipedia

The Sabah flag symbolizes the state's natural heritage and unity.

Best Time to Visit Sabah

  • Visit between March and October for drier weather, ideal for outdoor activities.
  • Avoid the monsoon season (November to February) if planning beach or island trips.

Source: Sabah Tourism Board

Getting Around Sabah

  • Long-distance buses connect major towns (KK to Sandakan, Tawau).
  • Rent a 4WD for exploring rural areas like Kinabatangan.
  • Domestic flights are efficient for traveling between regions.
Source: Lonely Planet Borneo

Accommodation Advice

  • Stay in Kota Kinabalu for urban conveniences and island access.
  • Choose nature lodges in Kinabatangan for wildlife viewing.
  • Select beach resorts in Tunku Abdul Rahman Marine Park.
  • Consider homestays in Kundasang for Mount Kinabalu views.
Source: Booking.com

Cultural Events & Seasons

  • Attend Kaamatan Festival (May) for harvest celebrations.
  • Visit during Sabah Fest (May) for cultural performances.
  • Time your trip with the Borneo Bird Festival (October)
  • Experience Regatta Lepa (April), a vibrant Bajau water village festival featuring decorated boats and traditional dances.
Source: Sabah Tourism
