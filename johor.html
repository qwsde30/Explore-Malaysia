A Glimpse into Johor's History

From royal beginnings to modern marvels, Johor is a land of tradition, progress, and cultural richness.

Timeline Highlights:
  • 1528 - Johor Sultanate established after the fall of Malacca.
  • 19th Century - Development under Temenggong Ibrahim and Sultan Abu Bakar, modernization and economic growth.
  • 1994 - Johor Bahru declared a city, becoming a key economic hub.
Historic Landmarks:
Istana Besar (Grand Palace)
Sultan Abu Bakar Mosque
Kota Tinggi Waterfalls
Arulmigu Sri Rajakaliamman Glass Temple
Iconic Cultural Festivals:
  • Johor Bahru Arts Festival
  • Zapin Dance Festival
  • Chinese New Year at Johor Bahru Old Temple
  • Hari Raya Aidilfitri at Istana Bukit Serene
Signature Cuisine:

Johor offers a unique blend of Malay, Chinese, and Indian flavors. Must-try dishes:

  • Laksa Johor
  • Otak-Otak
  • Lei-Cha
  • DimSum
  • KwayTeowKia

Essential Travel Tips for Johor

Make the most of your Johor adventure with these helpful tips

Best Time to Visit

  • The off-peak season in Johor is typically between March to May and September to November.
  • The weather is dry and sunny during this period.
  • It's ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.
Source: Tourism Johor

Food Scene

  • Johor offers a diverse and vibrant culinary experience with unique dishes like Laksa Johor and Murtabak.
  • Local eateries serve a variety of flavors, including favorites like Nasi Lemak and Teh Tarik.
  • Whether you're craving savory meals or refreshing desserts like Cendol, Johor has something to satisfy every taste.
Source: GoJohor

Shopping Suggestion

  • Johor offers a variety of shopping experiences, from luxury outlets to local markets.
  • Johor Premium Outlets is perfect for those looking for discounted branded goods, while Pasar Karat provides a unique flea market experience.
  • For a more modern shopping experience, head to Komtar JBCC, where you'll find international brands and entertainment options.
Source: Tripadvisor

Top Attraction

  • Johor boasts a range of top attractions, from the thrilling Legoland Malaysia to the tranquil beauty of Desaru Coast.
  • Visitors can explore historical sites like the Sultan Abu Bakar State Mosque or enjoy the natural wonders at Gunung Ledang.
  • Whether you're seeking adventure, culture, or relaxation, Johor offers something for everyone.
Source: Tourism Johor

Cultural Highlights

  • Johor is rich in cultural heritage, with landmarks like the Sultan Abu Bakar State Mosque showcasing its architectural splendor.
  • The Johor Old Chinese Temple is a significant symbol of the state's Chinese heritage, offering a glimpse into the past.
  • Visitors can also explore the Johor Heritage Trail, a journey through historical sites that tell the story of the region's diverse culture.
Source: Klook

Important Notes

  • When visiting Johor, it's important to plan your trip around the weather, as the monsoon season can affect outdoor activities.
  • Always carry local currency, as some smaller places may not accept credit cards.
  • Be mindful of local customs and etiquette, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Lastly, try to book accommodations and attractions in advance during peak seasons to avoid crowds and long wait times.
Source: Tripadvisor
